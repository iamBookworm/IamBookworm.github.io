Insurance

Life Insurance

A life insurance policy can provide financial support for your family after your death. Money from life insurance can replace your lost income and help pay normal living expenses in addition to medical bills, funeral and burial expenses, probate fees (fees associated with determining the validity of a will), and estate taxes.

Types

  • Term Life: It pays only if death occurs during the term of the policy, which is usually from one to 30 years. Most term policies have no other benefit provisions.
    • Level term means that the death benefit stays the same throughout the duration of the policy.
    • Decreasing term means that the death benefit drops, usually in one-year increments, over the course of the policy’s term.
  • Whole Life/Permanent Life: Whole life or permanent insurance pays a death benefit whenever the policyholder dies.
    • In the case of traditional whole life, both the death benefit and the premium are designed to stay the same (level) throughout the life of the policy.
    • Universal life, also known as adjustable life, allows more flexibility than traditional whole life policies. The savings vehicle (called a cash value account) generally earns a money market rate of interest.
    • Variable life policies combine death protection with a savings account that can be invested in stocks, bonds and money market mutual funds. The value of the policy may grow more quickly, but involves more risk. If investments do not perform well, the cash value and death benefit may decrease.

When Deciding What to Purchase

  1. Add up your normal living expenses, including things such as child care, food, utilities, gasoline, clothing, mortgage payments, rent, and college tuition. Consider also the cost of hospital bills, funeral services, and burial if you passed away.
  2. Estimate the amount of money your family would require each year, and multiply it by the number of years the insurance settlement would be needed.

Where to Purchase From?

  • An Insurance Agent
  • An Insurance Company

When buying life insurance, be sure to ask for quotes from at least three companies or agents. It would also be a good idea to request a net cost index from each of them to help you compare policies.

Note:Insurance companies often have three price classes, which are based on whether you are a smoker, nonsmoker, or someone with a history of very poor health.

How to File Life Insurance after Someone's Death

  • Contact the insurance agent or company and ask for help filling out the appropriate insurance claim forms.
  • Give the funeral director a certified copy of the death certificate, along with the claim on the policy.
  • The insurance company will evaluate the claim and offer a settlement. The claim can be recieved as one lump sum or multiple payments.

Car Insurance

Auto insurance is designed to compensate the policyholder if the car is damaged, stolen or will provide funds for medical bills.

Before Purchasing

  1. Research state’s minimum requirements so that you’re not purchasing what is unneeded
  2. Buy full coverage for financed(not fully-owned) cars
  3. Research among insurance companies.

Types of Coverage

  • Liability: Coverage required by NC laws. It covers bodily injury and property damage resulting from an accident.
  • Collision and Comprehensive: Collision means physical damage to your covered vehicle caused by an impact with another vehicle or object. Comprehensive covers other event such as theft or weather events.
  • Medical Payments: This coverage pays for reasonable and necessary medical and funeral expenses due to an automobile accident.

Health Insurance

Divided up into 4 metal categories:

  • Bronze: The insurance company pays 60% while purchaser pays 40%.
  • Silver: The insurance company pays 70% while purchaser pays 30%.
  • Gold: The insurance company pays 80% while purchaser pays 20%.
  • Platinum: The insurance company pays 90% while purchaser pays 10%.

Shopping for House Insurance Reference Sheet

Flood Insurance

  • Homeowners and Renters’ insurance often don’t cover flooding
  • Flood insurance covers direct physical losses from floods and losses resulting from flood related erosion caused by waves or currents of water exceeding anticipated cyclical levels and accompanied by a severe storm, flash flood, abnormal tide surge or a similar situation that results in flooding.
  • The maximum flood insurance coverage amount is $250,000 for the structure of the home and $100,000 for the contents of the home. ("Excess" coverage over and above the maximums that are available from NFIP is offered by private insurers.)
  • Flood losses for cars are covered under the optional, comprehensive portion of a standard automobile insurance policy.

Buying Flood Insurance

  • Federal flood insurance policies can be purchased directly from an insurance professional. Nearly 100 insurance companies write and service NFIP policies.
  • Flood insurance covers direct physical losses from floods and losses resulting from flood related erosion caused by waves or currents of water exceeding anticipated cyclical levels and accompanied by a severe storm, flash flood, abnormal tide surge or a similar situation that results in flooding.
  • There is a 30-day waiting period before a flood insurance policy takes effect.
  • It can be augmented with "excess" insurance – The NFIP policy maximums are inadequate to fully cover some people's assets so a growing number of private insurers have begun offering excess flood policies, intended to provide water damage protection to homeowners over and above the coverage provided by the NFIP policies.

Pet Insurance

Similar to Healh Insurance

  • Basic coverage is the least expensive option. Basic coverage provides the lowest reimbursements for procedures, and will help pay for accidental injuries, poisonings and illnesses (including cancer)
  • Comprehensive coverage is costlier than basic coverage, but offers more generous benefits, such as reimbursements for accidental injuries, emergencies and illnesses, and coverage for office visits, prescriptions, diagnostic tests, X-rays and lab fees.
  • Pet well care protection reimburses for preventive care, including physical exams, flea and heartworm prevention and vaccinations.

Renters Insurance

Similar to House Insurance

Includes three key types of financial protection:

  • Coverage for personal possessions
  • Liability protection
  • Additional living expenses (ALE)
    • The additional living expenses portion of your rental insurance policy pays for hotel bills, temporary rentals, restaurant meals and other expenses you incur while your rental home is being repaired or rebuilt.

The big difference is that renters insurance doesn't cover the building or structure of the apartment—that's the landlord's responsibility
