Basics - What You Should Know

Income and Expenses

Income is what can be earned or given in a period of time. It can come in the form of a paycheck or direct deposit if a person is working. Or as interest if a person was to invest in a stock.

Expenses are the cost of items that are consumed or used in the course of daily living. Such examples of expenses are TV and Internet Bills, Groceries, or Concert Tickets.

Budgeting

To manage income and expenses, it may be necessary to create a Budget but you don't want one like the two I'm about to list.

  1. Budget Surplus: There is more money being collected in an income than being spent on a daily basis. This is not a bad thing in general but is usually the result of being a cheapskate. Correcting this issue requires consuming the excess money or saving the excess money for later usage.
  2. Budget Deficit: There is too little cash being used on wants and needs of a person. It is not sustainable as it can lead to excess debt at a later time if borrowing is used to correct the issue. Two healthy ways to correct the issue is spending less or earning more.

What are Assets?

Assets are any item of economic value that can be converted to cash at a later time.

Some examples are:

  1. Cars
  2. Houses
  3. Jewelery

Overall, assets store wealth, increase income if used correctly, and can reduce expenses.

Borrowing

Borrowing is renting someone else's money for a period of time until the money can be repaid. If not repaid in a timely manner, interest can accumulate leading to additional money being spent.

Some examples of Borrowing are:

  1. Credit Cards
  2. Auto Loans
  3. Mortgage
  4. Student Loans
