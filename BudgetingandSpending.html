Basics - What You Should Know

What is Budgeting?

Budgeting is the process of creating a plan to spend your money(My Money Coach). This spending plan helps a person to determine ahead of time if he/she has enough money to cover their expenses. Budgeting ensures that a person will always have enough money leftover for the things he/she needs and wants.

How to Make a Budget

  1. Set Realistic Goals
    1. S.M.A.R.T Goals
      • Specific: Buy a car after you graduate.
      • Measurable: Have at least $2,500 for a down payment.
      • Achievable: Save $70 a month.
      • Relevant: You are passionate about the goal because you want to enjoy the freedom of a car for transportation.
      • Time-based: Reach your goal in 36 months, or three years.
  2. Identify your Income and Expenses
  3. Separate Wants and Needs
  4. Design Your Budget
  5. Put Your Plan into Action
  6. Prepare for Seasonal Expenses
  7. LOOK AHEAD

Budget Guidelines

  • Food(Groceries, Personal Care, and Baby Needs): 10-20%
  • Clothing(for all members of the family): 3-5%
  • Transportation(Bus, Taxi, Fuel, Insurance, Maintanence, and Parking): 15-20%
  • Housing(Mortgage, Taxes, Strata, Rent, Insurance, Hydro): 35%
  • Utilities(Phone, Cell Phone, Gas, Cable, and Internet): 5%
  • Medical(Health Care Premiums, Specialists, Over-the-Counter):3%
  • Debt Payments: 5-15%
  • Savings(Plan to save for later months): 5-10%
  • Personal(Entertainment, Recreation, Eating Out, Hobbies): 5-10%

Common Methods of Budgeting

  • Paper and pen: Collect receipts and record expenses
  • Excel Sheet: Track expenses using columns and rows
  • Online budget sites/apps
  • Envelope Method: Have an envelope for each of your expenses and insert cash in each envelope to track how much money you have for each expense.
